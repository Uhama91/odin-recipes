Ingredients for Lasagna (Serves 6-8)

For the Meat Sauce (Bolognese)

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 pound (450g) ground beef (or a mix of beef and pork)
  • 1/2 cup (120ml) dry red wine (optional, for depth of flavor)
  • 2 cans (14 oz/400g each) crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar (to balance acidity)
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • Fresh basil leaves (a small handful, chopped, for added freshness)

For the Béchamel Sauce

  • 4 tablespoons (60g) unsalted butter
  • 1/4 cup (30g) all-purpose flour
  • 3 cups (720ml) whole milk, warmed
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg (optional, for a classic touch)
  • Salt and white pepper, to taste

For the Lasagna Assembly

  • 12-15 lasagna noodles (pre-cooked or no-boil, depending on package instructions)
  • 2 cups (200g) shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1 cup (100g) grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 cup (250g) ricotta cheese (optional, for extra creaminess)
  • Fresh basil leaves (for garnish)

Steps to Cook Lasagna

Step 1: Prepare the Meat Sauce (Bolognese)

  1. Heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil in a large skillet or saucepan over medium heat.
  2. Add the finely chopped onion and sauté for 3-4 minutes until softened and translucent.
  3. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for another 1 minute until fragrant.
  4. Add the ground beef (and pork, if using) to the pan. Cook, breaking it up with a spoon, until browned and fully cooked (about 6-8 minutes). Drain excess fat if necessary.
  5. Pour in the red wine (if using) and let it simmer for 2-3 minutes to reduce slightly.
  6. Stir in the crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, dried oregano, dried basil, sugar, salt, and black pepper. Mix well.
  7. Reduce the heat to low, cover, and let the sauce simmer for 30-40 minutes, stirring occasionally, until thickened. Add chopped fresh basil in the last 5 minutes. Set aside to cool slightly.

Step 2: Prepare the Béchamel Sauce

  1. In a medium saucepan, melt 4 tablespoons of butter over medium heat.
  2. Whisk in 1/4 cup of flour and cook for 1-2 minutes to form a roux, stirring constantly to avoid burning.
  3. Gradually pour in the warmed milk while whisking continuously to prevent lumps. Cook for 5-7 minutes, stirring, until the sauce thickens and coats the back of a spoon.
  4. Season with nutmeg, salt, and white pepper. Remove from heat and set aside.

Step 3: Cook the Lasagna Noodles

  1. If using pre-cooked noodles, bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the lasagna noodles according to package instructions (usually 8-10 minutes) until al dente. Drain and rinse with cold water to prevent sticking. Lay them flat on a clean towel or parchment paper.
  2. If using no-boil noodles, you can skip this step and proceed directly to assembly.

Step 4: Preheat the Oven

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

Step 5: Assemble the Lasagna

  1. Spread a thin layer of meat sauce (about 1/2 cup) on the bottom of a 9x13-inch baking dish to prevent sticking.
  2. Place a layer of lasagna noodles (about 3-4 noodles, depending on size) over the sauce, slightly overlapping if needed.
  3. Spread 1/3 of the remaining meat sauce over the noodles.
  4. Spoon 1/3 of the béchamel sauce over the meat sauce, spreading it evenly.
  5. Sprinkle 1/3 of the mozzarella and Parmesan cheeses over the sauces. Add dollops of ricotta (if using) for extra creaminess.
  6. Repeat the layers (noodles, meat sauce, béchamel, cheeses) two more times, ending with a final layer of noodles.
  7. Top the final noodle layer with the remaining béchamel sauce, a generous layer of mozzarella, and a sprinkle of Parmesan for that golden crust.

Step 6: Bake the Lasagna

  1. Cover the dish with aluminum foil (tent it slightly to avoid sticking to the cheese) and bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes.
  2. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 15-20 minutes, or until the top is bubbly and golden brown.
  3. If desired, broil for 2-3 minutes at the end to get an extra crispy, cheesy top (watch closely to avoid burning).

Step 7: Rest and Serve

  1. Remove the lasagna from the oven and let it rest for 10-15 minutes to set, making it easier to slice.
  2. Garnish with fresh basil leaves for a pop of color and flavor.
  3. Cut into squares and serve warm, perhaps with a side of garlic bread or a green salad.
