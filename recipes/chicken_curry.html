Chicken Curry Recipe

Home

Imagine tender pieces of chicken simmered in a fragrant, creamy curry sauce infused with warm spices, garlic, and fresh ginger. The rich aroma of turmeric, cumin, and coriander fills the air, mingling with the sweetness of coconut milk and the tang of ripe tomatoes. Served over fluffy basmati rice, this dish is a comforting and flavorful classic that brings warmth to every bite.

Ingredients for Chicken Curry (Serves 4-6)

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 1.5 pounds (700g) boneless, skinless chicken breast or thighs, cut into pieces
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 can (14 oz/400g) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 can (14 oz/400ml) coconut milk
  • 1/2 cup (120ml) chicken broth or water
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • Fresh cilantro leaves, for garnish
  • Cooked basmati rice, for serving

Steps to Cook Chicken Curry

Step 1: Sauté the Aromatics

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large pan over medium heat.
  2. Add the chopped onion and sauté for 3-4 minutes until soft and translucent.
  3. Stir in the minced garlic and grated ginger, cooking for another minute until fragrant.

Step 2: Cook the Chicken

  1. Add the chicken pieces to the pan and cook until lightly browned on all sides.
  2. Sprinkle the turmeric, cumin, coriander, garam masala, and chili powder over the chicken.
  3. Stir well to coat the chicken evenly with the spices.

Step 3: Simmer the Curry

  1. Pour in the crushed tomatoes and chicken broth, stirring to combine.
  2. Bring the mixture to a gentle simmer and cook for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  3. Add the coconut milk, mixing well, and let the curry simmer for another 10 minutes until the sauce thickens.
  4. Season with salt and black pepper to taste.

Step 4: Serve

  1. Remove from heat and let the curry rest for a few minutes.
  2. Garnish with fresh cilantro leaves.
  3. Serve hot over basmati rice.
